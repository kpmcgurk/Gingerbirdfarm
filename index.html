About

Nestled in the Richland Valley of Goshen, Arkansas, our organic vegetable farm is dedicated to regenerative agriculture and community connection. We cultivate a diverse array of seasonal produce using practices that enrich the soil and support local biodiversity.

Our commitment to organic methods ensures that every vegetable we grow is free from harmful chemicals, ensuring healthy food for our neighbors while maintaining ecosystem balance. You can find our produce at the Goshen Farmers Market on Thursday evenings from May through October, or contact us directly for special orders

Free Range Eggs

Our farm's fertility relies on composting, and our small flock of chickens play a critical role in this process by scratching to break down our inputs into rich compost. This process combined with fresh pasture during the growing season and suplemented with grain provides nutritious eggs that we are proud to offer to our customers.

Honey and Beeswax

>

Our first season growing on our property, we struggled with pollination. Catfaced tomatos, deformed cucumbers and blanched blackberries were normal. We introduced honeybees to the farm to assist our rebounding native pollinators, and have never looked back. Our bees forage area includes fertile valleys of the upper White River to the wild Ozark Foothills; bringing a florally diverse nectar and pollen with rich flavor. Our honey is never heated, never filtered and only available seasonally, July until sold out.

Gourmet Garlic & Seed

All winter long, our garlic ais nestled in rich soil setting roots to produce bold, aromatic bulbs in the spring. Grown without synthetic chemicals right here in Arkansas, each clove delivers a robust, spicy bite that mellows into a deep, savory richness. Harvested at the peak of freshness, our garlic retains its intense taste; perfect for elevating any dish into the ranks of Michelin territory. We specialize in the Hardneck varieties of Music and German Extra Hardy and sell Scapes in mid spring, cured bulbs in mid summer and limited seed stock starting in August.

Dairy Goats

Goats joined our farm in 2023 as natural partners in managing invasive broadleaf vegetation; no herbicides needed. Thanks to their steady work, we’ve been able to reclaim land from aggressive species like honeysuckle, privet, and multiflora rose, making space for native plants to thrive and restore ecological balance. In return, our Nigerian Dwarf herd provides a sweet, creamy milk that’s high in butterfat and delightfully free of the typical “goaty” flavor. Available in very limited quantities during the milking season. Contact us to join the waitlist.
